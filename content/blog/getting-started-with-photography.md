Every photographer starts somewhere. For me, it began with a borrowed camera and an afternoon walk through the city.

## Finding Your Eye

The hardest part of photography is not technical — it is learning to see. The camera is just a tool. What matters is noticing the light falling through a window, the shadow cutting across a wall, the moment between moments.

## Keep It Simple

When you are starting out, constraints are your friend. Shoot with one lens. Walk the same route at different times of day. Pay attention to what draws your eye and why.

The work will evolve from there.
